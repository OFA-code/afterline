# 15-minute Revenue Leak Audit (sales call script)

You are diagnosing, not pitching. They should feel understood before price.

## Opening (2 min)

> "Thanks for hopping on. I'll keep this to 15 minutes. I'm going to ask how leads come in and what happens when you're on a job or after hours. At the end I'll tell you honestly if we're a fit — and if not, I'll say so."

## Discovery (8 min)

1. "Where do most leads come from?" (Google, Angi, referrals, ads)
2. "What happens when you miss a call during a job?"
3. "How fast does someone text back today — honestly?"
4. "How many open estimates are sitting waiting right now?"
5. "How many Google reviews in the last 90 days?"
6. "If you booked one more job a week from follow-up alone, what would that be worth?"

**Write numbers.** Average job value: $____. Jobs per week lost: ____.

## Math moment (3 min)

> "You said an average job is about $[X]. If we recover one job a month from faster follow-up, that's $[X]. Our pilot is $497 for 30 days. That's not a cost — that's insurance on leads you already bought."

## Offer (2 min)

> "We run three things: missed-call text-back, estimate follow-up, and review requests after jobs. AI drafts, you approve templates once, you get a Sunday report. Pilot is $497 for 30 days. We measure week one vs week four. If we miss our response-time SLA, we fix it before you pay again."

## Close

> "Want to start pilot next Monday? I'll send a one-page agreement today."

**If no:** "What would need to be true for this to be a yes in 30 days?"

## Disqualify fast (ethical)

Say no if:

- Fewer than 10 leads/month
- Owner won't approve templates
- They want you to run ads instead
