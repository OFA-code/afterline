# Ethics — how we charge without ripping people off

## Principle

We only make money when the client makes more money than they pay us. If we can't point to a number, we don't sell.

## What we promise (and deliver)

| Promise | Reality |
|---------|---------|
| Faster follow-up | Measurable: time-to-first-text after missed call |
| More reviews | Count Google reviews before/after |
| More rebooked estimates | Track pipeline stages we touch |
| Your time back | Owner stops playing phone tag |

## What we never do

- Fake reviews or astroturfing
- Spam people who opted out
- Lock clients into contracts they can't read
- Hide that AI drafts messages (we disclose; humans approve templates)
- Guarantee specific revenue (we guarantee **process** and **response times**, not lottery outcomes)

## Pricing ethics

- **Pilot month** at a discount with a written success checklist — if we miss SLAs, they don't pay month 2 until fixed
- **Month-to-month** after pilot — no 12-month handcuffs on v1
- **Clear scope:** what's included vs add-on (ads, website rebuild, etc. are out of scope)

## Refund / exit

- 14-day exit after any month if we failed documented SLAs two weeks in a row
- Export their sequences and data — no hostage-taking

## Sales ethics

- We only pitch businesses where our math works: **average job value × close rate lift > 3× our fee**
- If they're too small (owner does everything, no CRM, no leads), we say no and refer them elsewhere

This document is your spine when you feel pressure to over-promise. Under-promise on outcomes, over-deliver on speed and reporting.
